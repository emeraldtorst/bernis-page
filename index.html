The Problem We're Solving

Vienna's excellent waste management system can be confusing, especially for international residents. Language barriers and different recycling practices from home countries lead to contamination of recyclables, making the whole process less effective and more expensive.

Our project bridges this gap by providing clear, accessible information in English to help everyone contribute to Vienna's sustainability goals.

About Our Project

Our Mission

To reduce contamination in Vienna's recycling streams by providing clear guidelines and education in multiple languages, starting with English.

Our Approach

Community-driven solutions developed with input from international residents, local waste management experts, and sustainability advocates.

Our Goals

1. Reduce recycling contamination by 30% in target neighborhoods
2. Engage 5,000 international residents in proper waste sorting
3. Expand to other languages by 2024

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I recycle pizza boxes in Vienna?

Yes, but only if they're clean. Greasy or food-stained parts should go in residual waste.

Where can I dispose of old electronics?

Electronics can be taken to any of Vienna's 48 Problemstoff-Sammelzentren (hazardous waste collection centers).

What plastics are recyclable in Vienna?

Only plastic bottles (PET) and packaging marked with recycling codes 1, 2, 4, and 5 go in the yellow bins.

Quick Waste Sorting Guide

  • Residual Waste (Black Bin): Non-recyclable waste, diapers, cigarette butts
  • Yellow Bin/Box: Plastic packaging, metal, beverage cartons (clean)
  • Paper (Blue Bin): Newspapers, cardboard, paper packaging
  • Glass (White/Green Bins): Glass bottles and jars (separated by color)
  • Organic Waste (Brown Bin): Food scraps, garden waste, biodegradable materials
